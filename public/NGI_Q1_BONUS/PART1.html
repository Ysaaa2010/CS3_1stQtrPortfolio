My Favorite Recipe: Tinola

Tinola is one of my comfort foods. I can eat it for breakfast, lunch, dinner, and as a snack. I love tinola. Tinola loves me. I love it because it reminds me of mama's cooking and my hometown, Pangasinan.

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs. chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 2 green papaya, wedged
  • 1 ½ cup malunggay leaves(my mom usually adds 2 because i love dis)
  • 5 cloves garlic, crushed and chopped
  • 1 onions, chopped
  • 3 thumbs ginger, julienned
  • 2 Maggi Magic Chicken Cubes
  • 1 quart rice wash
  • 1 quart water, as needed
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pot. Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger until fragrant.
  2. Sauté the garlic, onion, and ginger and let them mingle until the onion gets all soft and sweet.
  3. Time to add the chicken! Toss those pieces into the pot and cook for about 2 minutes, or until they turn a lovely light brown.
  4. Pour in 2 tablespoons of fish sauce and stir well.
  5. Add rice wash and bring to a boil. Reduce to a simmer until chicken is tender.
  6. Add the sliced papaya and cook until soft.
  7. Add the Maggi Magic Chicken Cubes and stir.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Add chili malunggay leaves and simmer for another 1–2 minutes.
  10. Serve hot with steamed rice.
  11. Happy eating! yum yum.
